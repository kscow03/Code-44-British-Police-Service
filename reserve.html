Reserve Operator Programme

The Reserve Operator Programme allows officers to remain within their primary department whilst providing additional support to the Armed Response Unit when required.

Reserve Operators play an important role in strengthening operational capability, supporting deployments and developing specialist firearms skills alongside their normal duties.

Role Expectations

  • Maintain professional standards at all times
  • Attend mandatory firearms training
  • Remain operationally deployable
  • Support ARU operations when required
  • Maintain activity within your primary department
  • Demonstrate sound judgement and teamwork

Training Commitment

All Reserve Operators must complete the required firearms training and maintain competence through regular continuation training exercises.

  • Initial Firearms Course
  • Continuation Training
  • Scenario-Based Exercises
  • Operational Assessments
  • Qualification Maintenance

Benefits of the Programme

  • Specialist firearms experience
  • Additional operational opportunities
  • Professional development
  • Leadership experience
  • Pathway to future full-time opportunities

Selection Process

  1. Application Submission
  2. Command Team Review
  3. Interview / Assessment
  4. Training Phase
  5. Operational Qualification

Before You Apply

Applicants should carefully consider the additional responsibilities of the programme. Reserve Operators are expected to balance their primary department commitments whilst maintaining the standards required of specialist firearms personnel.
